Professional Self-Assessment

Throughout my time in the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University, I have developed a strong foundation in software development, problem solving, and system design. Over the course of nearly five years, I have gained experience working with multiple programming languages including Java, Python, and C++, as well as tools such as Android Studio and MongoDB. These experiences have helped me grow both technically and professionally.

One of the most important skills I have developed is my understanding of the software development life cycle. Through multiple projects, I have learned how to plan, design, implement, and refine software solutions while considering user needs and system requirements. I have also strengthened my ability to write clean, well-structured, and maintainable code, which is essential for working in a professional development environment.

Collaboration and communication have also played an important role in my development. While working on projects and assignments, I have learned how to present technical ideas clearly and effectively to different audiences. This includes explaining design decisions, documenting code, and reflecting on my work in a way that others can understand. These skills are especially important when working with teams or communicating with stakeholders.

In addition to software development, I have gained experience with data structures, algorithms, and database management. I have learned how to design efficient solutions, evaluate trade-offs, and implement improvements that enhance performance and reliability. My work with MongoDB and database-driven applications has also helped me understand the importance of data integrity, validation, and secure data handling practices.

Security has become an important consideration in my approach to development. I have learned to think more critically about how applications handle data and how vulnerabilities can be introduced if proper precautions are not taken. This includes validating user input, protecting sensitive information, and considering how systems might be misused or exploited.

The artifacts included in this ePortfolio represent my growth across three key areas: software design and engineering, algorithms and data structures, and databases. Each artifact demonstrates not only the functionality of the original project, but also the enhancements I implemented to improve the system and better align it with real-world expectations. These enhancements allowed me to apply what I have learned throughout the program in a more practical and meaningful way.

Overall, this ePortfolio reflects my development as a programmer and highlights the practical skills I have built in preparation for a career in software development. As I move forward, I plan to continue building on these skills, expanding my knowledge of full-stack development and cloud technologies, and applying what I have learned in a professional setting.
